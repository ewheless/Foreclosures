NYC Neighborhood Foreclosures

By Erika Wheless

March 11, 2020

Vacant storefronts are becoming a common sight accross NYC boroughs. According to data from the Furman Center at NYU, the number of foreclosures accross NYC are becoming more common, too.

Foreclosure means that the homeowner has stopped making mortgage payments on their home, and so the bank takes possession of the home. This process starts with a notice of foreclosure, which is usually issued after 90 days of missed mortgage payments. The data in this article will look at the total number of residential properties that had mortgage foreclosure actions initiated against them.

In the state of New York, residents are sent a 90 day notice before being foreclosed on. Sixty eight percent of all NYC neighborhoods had more foreclosures in 2018 than in 2000, but each neighborhood had fewer foreclosures in 2018 than in 2010, a good sign that many went back down after the Financial Crisis.

Foreclosures spiked in 2007-2009 due to the Financial Crisis. In some boroughs, the neighborhood with the most foreclosures before during and after the Crisis, some boroughs had the neighborhood change.

In Brooklyn, From 2001-2006, Bedstuy had the most foreclosures with an average of 506 over those 5 years. Then in 2007, 2008, and 2010, East New York had the most, jumping to 910, 860, and 939 foreclosures respectively. In 2009, and from 2011 on, Flatlands/Canarsie took the lead, with an average of 776 foreclosures from 2011 to 2018.

In The Bronx, Williamsbridge/Baychester has consistently been the neightborhood with the most foreclosures, with an average of 464 per year from 2000-2018. The year with the most foreclosures was in 2013, with 738.

Manhattan has the least amount of foreclosures, with an enviable average of just 32 foreclosures from 2000-2018. The neighborhood with the most is Central Harlem (average of 65 from 2000-2008). But foreclosures peaked in Midtown in 2009, leaping to 286 foreclosures.

Queens has the highest number of foreclosures in all five boroughs with an average of 313 for all of Queens from 2000 to 2018. Jamaica/Hollis is the hardest hit, well above other Queens neighborhoods. Jamaica averages 1263 foreclosures from 2000-2018, peaking at 2136 in 2009.

In Staten Island, St. George/Stapelton is consistently the area with the most foreclosures, averaging 584 from 2000-2018.
